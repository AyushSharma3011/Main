Major Issues of Climate Change

    Global Warming

  • Global warming is the long-term warming of the planet's overall temperature. Though this warming trend has been going on for a long time, its pace has significantly increased in the last hundred years due to the burning of fossil fuels. As the human population has increased, so has the volume of fossil fuels burned.
  • Curing global warming requires urgent, collective action. Transitioning to renewable energy, reforestation, and sustainable practices are crucial. Governments, industries, and individuals must collaborate to reduce carbon emissions, invest in green technologies, and prioritize conservation. Public awareness and policy changes are vital to creating a sustainable, resilient future for our planet.

    Melting Ice Caps

  • Polar ice caps are melting as global warming causes climate change. We lose Arctic sea ice at a rate of almost 13% per decade, and over the past 30 years, the oldest and thickest ice in the Arctic has declined by a stunning 95%.
  • Mitigating melting ice caps demands urgent global action. Reduce greenhouse gas emissions through renewable energy adoption, sustainable practices, and conservation. Strengthen international cooperation for climate agreements and policies. Invest in research for ice cap preservation strategies. Raise public awareness to drive individual and collective efforts in protecting these vital ecosystems, securing a sustainable future.

Land Slides

  • A landslide is a mass movement of material, such as rock, earth or debris, down a slope. They can happen suddenly or more slowly over long periods of time. When the force of gravity acting on a slope exceeds the resisting forces of a slope, the slope will fail and a landslide occurs.
  • To safeguard against landslides, implement effective land-use planning, avoiding construction on steep slopes. Install retaining walls, erosion control measures, and proper drainage systems. Conduct regular slope stability assessments, and reinforce vulnerable areas with vegetation. Promote community awareness and early warning systems to mitigate risks, ensuring the safety of lives and property.
